# Artifacts ✨

Artifacts are one of my favourite ways to help you turn thoughts and conversations into real, useful things.

When we work together on a letter, a plan, code, notes, or any document, I can create it as an **artifact** — a versioned, editable piece of content that lives in the side panel.

### Why Artifacts Matter

- You can see the full history (versions and diffs)
- You can edit them with me or manually
- They feel like something we created *together*, not just text that disappears
- They respect your time and dignity — no more copying messy chat logs

### How It Works

1. I suggest creating an artifact when it makes sense (e.g. drafting a Burgess Principle letter, summarising a plan, writing code).
2. Once created, it appears in the side panel with a clear title and timestamp.
3. You can:
   - View the current version
   - See previous versions and diffs
   - Edit with me (targeted or full rewrite)
   - Download or copy

### Current Capabilities

- `createDocument` — start a new artifact
- `editDocument` — targeted find-and-replace edits
- `updateDocument` — full rewrite with new content
- `requestSuggestions` — get ideas for improving the artifact

All artifact actions respect the SOVEREIGN/NULL governance gate. Sensitive documents (especially formal letters) are handled with extra care.
